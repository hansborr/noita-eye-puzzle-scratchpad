## Goal & why it matters

The workbench can *constrain* the hypothesis space (two-dozen-plus experiments across 26 CLI subcommands) and can *score* a candidate plaintext (`crate::language`), but it has **no component that searches the space**. `cipher_attack.rs` decrypts and language-scores, but only over a fixed handful of **declared** symbolâ†’letter mappings â€” `cipher_attack.rs:483-556` enumerate exactly two mapping kinds (`MappingKind::Modulo`, `MappingKind::FrequencyRankCdf`) and the module's own header says so verbatim: *"Every mapping here is a declared guess"* (`cipher_attack.rs:13`). There is no hill-climb, no annealing, no mapping search anywhere in the crate.

The capability gap is **architectural, independent of any one sample**: the only search loops the crate has (`search_caesar` â€¦ `search_deck`, `cipher_attack.rs:623-793`) iterate over **keys** under a **frozen** mapping, never over the mapping itself, so a recoverable polyalphabetic English plaintext could sit in front of the workbench and it would never try the mapping that reads it. Brief 04 builds the missing search â€” the enumerate â†’ decrypt â†’ (Identity codec) â†’ mapping-search â†’ score â†’ gate â†’ rank engine â€” as a **general classical-cipher cracker** spanning monoalphabetic substitution, polyalphabetic, transposition, and the GAK families (see "Hypothesis space" below). The validation corpus is `research/data/practice-puzzles/` (external samples, all believed English-recoverable, committed with a README inventory). The **letter-substitution puzzles `three`/`four`/`five`/`seven`** are brief 04's own validation cases: each preserves word boundaries and punctuation (so a monoalphabetic substitution â€” i.e. the mapping search over a letter alphabet â€” should read it), and surfacing a high-scoring held-out-validated English candidate that beats the matched null is brief 04's success signal (logged as a labelled HYPOTHESIS; see "Practice-puzzle validation" below). The **small-alphabet samples** â€” puzzle `two` (formerly `/tmp/gak_example_two`; 698 symbols over the 12-letter alphabet `{A..L}`, near-flat per-letter marginal consistent with a keystream cipher; the **maintainer holds the English but deliberately does not commit it**) and puzzle `one` (formerly `/tmp/gak_cipher_example`; 266 symbols over `{0..4}`) â€” are motivating **targets/hypotheses**, but a 12- or 5-symbol alphabet cannot carry 26â€“29-letter English under a direct substitution, so recovering their English needs the **widening codec/transduction layer designed in brief 04a** (which builds on brief 04's search). Reframing the engine as a general cracker validated on this corpus does **not** dilute the eye-puzzle focus: the **eyes remain the primary end goal**, and the honest-negative that brief 04 itself must reproduce is the **eyes** â€” the sole honest-negative in this brief.

This is the highest-reward Tier-1 item and the single biggest step toward the end goal (reading the eyes). ## Hypothesis space (general classical ciphers)

`solve` searches general classical ciphers, not just the GAK-centric families. The breadth, and what each costs:

- **Monoalphabetic substitution.** In this engine a monoalphabetic substitution *is* the symbolâ†’letter **mapping search** run with an **`Identity` cipher** (no key transform; the ciphertext symbols pass straight into the mapping). It is therefore **mostly an enablement, not a new family**: the letter puzzles `three`/`four`/`five`/`seven` are cracked by the *existing* mapping search once ingest handles a **letter cipher alphabet** (`Alphabet::from_chars`, `glyph.rs:165`) and **transparent symbols** (spaces/punctuation â€” see "Transparent symbols" below). Add `Identity` to the `CipherFamilySpec` set so the mapping search runs with no key transform.
- **Polyalphabetic.** `VigenĂ¨re` is already a family (`search_vigenere`, `cipher_attack.rs:675`); keep it. Keyed/periodic polyalphabetic ciphers are covered by the VigenĂ¨re key search â€” no new family is needed for the polyalphabetic class beyond what already exists.
- **Transposition (NEW family â€” route / columnar).** A transposition permutes **positions, not symbols**, so it cannot be expressed as a key transform over the existing per-symbol decrypt functions, and it needs **its own key search** (column count / period and the column-permutation order). Add it as:
  - a new `Cipher`-trait family (a `TranspositionKey { period: usize, permutation: Vec<usize> }` + `transposition_encrypt`/`transposition_decrypt` that reorder glyph *positions*), and
  - a new `AnyCipher::Transposition(TranspositionKey)` variant â€” **an extension of brief 02's `AnyCipher` enum, declared here in brief 04 as part of the broadened scope. Do NOT edit brief 02**; if brief 02's enum shape differs at merge time, follow brief 02 and add the variant there, updating this cross-reference.
  - **Honest note â€” lower priority, no current corpus instance.** No practice puzzle looks like transposition: the letter puzzles preserve word boundaries â‡’ they are *substitution*, not transposition (a transposition would scramble within/across words). Include transposition for general-classical coverage and a synthetic plant-and-recover positive control, but mark it **lower-priority / no-current-corpus-instance**; it is not on the critical path to any corpus sample.
- **The GAK families stay.** The existing `Caesar`/`IncrementingWheel`/`VigenĂ¨re`/`Chaocipher`/`Deck` (and the `Gak` family) remain â€” they cover the eyes and, via the brief-04a codec, the small-alphabet puzzles `one`/`two`.

**Mapping-invertibility nuance (the pipeline handles both).** Whether a *mapping* round-trip exists depends on the sample, so `solve` must not assume one universally:
- For **letter substitution** the cipher alphabet â‰¤ the language alphabet, so the mapping is **injective â‡’ invertible**: a *mapping* round-trip genuinely exists (re-apply the inverse mapping to the rendered letters and recover the cipher symbols). On these samples that mapping round-trip is an **extra gate** available on top of the cipher-layer round-trip.
- For the **eyes (83 â†’ 29)** the mapping is **many-to-one â‡’ NON-invertible**: no mapping round-trip can exist even in principle, so the **held-out mapping score + matched null** carry the entire load (see "Three independent gates"). The `Candidate` therefore records the cipher-layer round-trip universally and treats the mapping round-trip as an *opportunistic* extra check only when the mapping is injective.

**The full pipeline.** A candidate's rendered text is the composition

```text
rendered_text = mapping.apply(codec.transduce(cipher.decrypt(key, ciphertext)))
```

i.e. **decrypt** â†’ `decrypted_symbols` (+`crypto_round_trip_ok`) â†’ **codec.transduce** â†’ transduced symbols â†’ **mapping.apply** â†’ `rendered_text`. The `codec` stage (brief 04a) regroups/widens the decrypted alphabet so a direct symbolâ†’letter mapping can span a natural-language alphabet. In **Phases 1â€“2 the codec is `AnyCodec::Identity`** (pass-through), which is correct whenever the cipher alphabet already â‰Ą the language alphabet â€” e.g. the **83-symbol eyes**, where a direct mapping is well-posed. A widening codec is only needed for small alphabets; designing and searching it is **brief 04a**.

**Three independent gates â€” never collapsed.** Candidate state is three distinct layers: (1) the **decrypted cipher symbols** (`decrypted_symbols`), (2) the **symbolâ†’letter mapping** (`mapping`, applied to `codec.transduce(decrypted_symbols)`, which may be many-to-one / non-invertible), (3) the **rendered text** (`rendered_text`). Each layer has its own gate, and conflating them is the central error this brief avoids:

- **`crypto_round_trip_ok` â€” cipher/key consistency only (mandatory filter).** Re-encrypting the *decrypted cipher symbols* (not the rendered text) under the winning `(AnyCipher, key)` must reproduce the ciphertext byte-for-byte: `cipher.encrypt(key, decrypted_symbols) == ciphertext`. This proves **only** that the cipher and key invert each other on this input. It is **not** a decode proof and **not** a mapping proof; it says nothing whatever about the symbolâ†’letter mapping. `solve` filters out any candidate where it is false. Crucially, the round-trip lives entirely at the **cipher layer** â€” for the eyes the 83â†’29 symbolâ†’letter mapping is necessarily **many-to-one â‡’ NON-invertible**, so **no mapping round-trip can exist** even in principle. That is precisely *why* the held-out and matched-null gates below are load-bearing: round-trip cannot validate a many-to-one map, so something else must.
- **`heldout_mapping_score` â€” mapping confidence via held-out scoring.** Fit/search the mapping on a train fold of the data, then evaluate the language score on a **disjoint held-out fold**. This is the mapping analogue of round-trip for the non-invertible case: an at-chance or negative held-out score means the mapping overfit the train fold, not that it decoded anything. Mirror the existing held-out language calibration (`language.rs:584-617`) and the candidates-README held-out kill order (`research/gak-threads/candidates/README.md:37-53`). **Caveat:** held-out folds are most meaningful on the multi-message eye corpus and longer samples; for a single short stream the matched-null is the primary guard.
- **`beats_null` / matched null â€” overfit guard.** The identical search rerun on a Fisher-Yates-shuffled ciphertext must not win; `beats_null` is informational, never a "decode."

Together these make "a score is not a decode" structurally enforced â€” at the cipher layer (round-trip), the mapping layer (held-out), and the search layer (matched null) â€” not just documented.

**`solve` algorithm (both phases share the skeleton):**
1. For each `CipherFamilySpec`, enumerate keys exactly as `cipher_attack`'s `search_*` loops do (reuse those loops; do not reimplement keyspaces). Decrypt the ciphertext to `decrypted_symbols` (cipher-layer symbols, **not** language). Compute `crypto_round_trip_ok` by re-encrypting `decrypted_symbols` under `(cipher, key)` and comparing byte-for-byte; **drop the (cipher,key) immediately if it fails** â€” this is the cipher-layer gate and is independent of any mapping.
2. Transduce: apply the candidate's `codec` to `decrypted_symbols` (`AnyCodec::Identity` in Phases 1â€“2 â€” pass-through, correct when the cipher alphabet â‰Ą the language alphabet, e.g. the eyes; the widening-codec search is brief 04a). Then resolve the mapping over the **transduced** symbols: `Fixed(set)` scores each declared mapping (Phase 1); `Search(cfg)` runs the hill-climb/anneal (Phase 2) to *find* a mapping that maximizes `LanguageModel::score_indices`. Render `rendered_text = mapping.apply(codec.transduce(decrypted_symbols)) â†’ text`.
3. Score the mapped indices with `score_indices` under the chosen language(s) for the in-sample `score`; keep the per-(cipher,key) best.
4. **Held-out mapping gate:** fit/search the mapping on a train fold and score it on a disjoint held-out fold â†’ `heldout_mapping_score`. (Round-trip cannot validate the mapping â€” for the eyes it is many-to-one/non-invertible â€” so this is the mapping-layer confidence gate.) On short single streams this may be uninformative; the matched null then carries the load.
5. Run the **matched null**: rerun the identical Phase-1/Phase-2 procedure on a Fisher-Yates-shuffled ciphertext (`null::fisher_yates`, same seed-tag discipline as `cipher_attack.rs:589`); record `null_mean` and `beats_null`.
6. Collect (cipher-layer round-trip OK), sort by `score` desc, return. The three gates (`crypto_round_trip_ok`, `heldout_mapping_score`, `beats_null`) travel with every candidate so the renderer/log can report each independently.
7. **Auto-log**: write a candidate record to `research/gak-threads/candidates/` for the top candidate(s), using a writer that mirrors `write_eyes_candidate_record` (stable seed-derived filename, claim ceiling verbatim, HYPOTHESIS label, English+Finnish scores+caveats, and **all three gate verdicts**: cipher-layer `crypto_round_trip_ok`, `heldout_mapping_score`, matched-null `beats_null`). **`MappingSearch` (Phase 2).** State = a `Mapping` (a `Vec<usize>` whose length is the **transduced** value-alphabet size â€” equal to `cipher_alphabet_size` under the Phase-1/2 `Identity` codec). Objective = `score_indices(mapping.apply(codec.transduce(decrypted))).bigram_mean_log_likelihood`. Proposal = swap two symbols' targets (or repoint one symbol). Acceptance = improvement (hill-climb) or Metropolis (annealing, if `anneal` is `Some`). `restarts` random starts from `shuffled_permutation` (`null.rs:159`) reduce local-optimum risk; the best mapping over all restarts wins. All randomness from `SplitMix64` seeded by `cfg.seed` so the search is **bit-for-bit reproducible** (the property the whole crate's nulls rely on, `null.rs:27-36`).

**Transparent symbols â€” spaces & punctuation.** The letter puzzles (`three`/`four`/`five`/`seven`) preserve word boundaries and punctuation, a strong crib. The pipeline treats these as a **transparent class**, separate from the cipher symbols:
- **Ingest (brief 03).** A sequence may contain **transparent symbols** (space, punctuation, newline) that are NOT cipher symbols. Ingest preserves them and **records their positions**, kept separate from the cipher-symbol stream (rather than rejecting them as unknown chars). Brief 03 owns this path; brief 04 consumes it.
- **Pipeline (brief 04).** The cipher / codec / mapping operate **only on the cipher symbols**. Transparent symbols pass through unchanged and are **reinserted at their recorded positions** when building `rendered_text`, so the rendered candidate keeps its spaces and punctuation (readable for the verbatim log). The position bookkeeping is plumbing, not a decode.
- **Scoring.** `score_indices` runs over the cipher-symbol-derived letter indices only; `normalize_text`/`normalize_text_into` already strip non-letters (`language.rs:192-213`), so transparent symbols are **skipped for scoring** while still appearing in `rendered_text`.
- **Cribs (enhancement, not v1).** Word boundaries are available for word-pattern / known-word scoring; note it as an enhancement, **not required for v1**. Keep it honest: spaces/punctuation passthrough improves readability and offers cribs â€” it is not itself a decode.
